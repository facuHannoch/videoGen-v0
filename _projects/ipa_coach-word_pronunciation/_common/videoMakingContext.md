# Phonemes explanations

- /iː/

Explanation: spread your lips slightly, keep your tongue high and forward, and hold the vowel a little longer.

- /ɪ/

Explanation: Keep your lips relaxed, and open your mouth slightly.

- /ɛ/

Explanation: keep your lips relaxed, open your mouth slightly, and place your tongue forward at a middle height.

- /æ/

Explanation: open your mouth fairly wide, keep your lips slightly spread (not rounded), place the tip of your tongue just behind your lower front teeth, and push the middle of your tongue forward and low in your mouth. keep the sound short and relaxed, letting the jaw stay open while the voice comes out.

- /ɔ/

Explanation: round your lips slightly, open your mouth moderately, keep the back of your tongue raised toward the back of the mouth, and hold the vowel a little longer while letting the voice flow smoothly.

- /ʌ/

Explanation: relax your lips and keep them neutral, not rounded. Open your mouth slightly. Place your tongue in the center of your mouth, neither high nor low, and keep it relaxed and slightly pulled back. Let the sound come out naturally from your voice.

- /ɑ/

Explanation: open your mouth wide and relaxed, keep your lips neutral (not rounded), and place your tongue low and toward the back of your mouth. let the sound flow out smoothly and hold the vowel slightly longer.

- /u/

Explanation: round your lips forward as if you are gently blowing out a candle. keep your tongue high and pulled toward the back of your mouth. leave only a small opening between the tongue and the roof of the mouth. keep the lips rounded and hold the sound slightly longer while letting your voice flow out smoothly.

- /ə/

Explanation: relax your mouth and keep your lips in a neutral position. let your jaw open slightly. keep your tongue relaxed in the middle of your mouth, neither high nor low and not touching the roof or teeth. let air flow out gently while your vocal cords vibrate, producing a very short, soft “uh” sound.

- /ɝ/

Explanation: relax your lips in a neutral position, slightly open your mouth, and raise the middle of your tongue toward the roof of your mouth while curling the tip slightly back. Keep the tongue tense and let your voice vibrate as the sound comes out.

- /p/

Explanation: close your lips completely and build a little air pressure behind them. keep your tongue relaxed and not touching the roof of the mouth. then release the lips quickly so the air pops out.

- /d/

Explanation: place the tip of your tongue lightly against the ridge just behind your upper teeth, keep your lips relaxed, then release the tongue quickly while turning your voice on.
